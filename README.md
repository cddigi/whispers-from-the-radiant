## üìñ About the Game

The Prime Radiant has detected a critical divergence in the Seldon Plan. As rival Second Foundation mentalics, you each receive different whispered calculations from the Prime Radiant. Through 13 tricks representing probability nodes in psychohistory, you must prove your interpretation will best resolve the crisis.

But beware the paradox of power: winning too many tricks (10-13) reveals your manipulation and voids your calculations, scoring zero points. The most subtle influence wins.

### Game Features

- **Mental Shields**: Cards are hidden behind psychohistorical equations until revealed
- **Hold-to-Reveal**: Press and hold to temporarily pierce your opponent's mental shield
- **Special Abilities**: Six unique card powers that alter the flow of play
- **Character Abilities**: Each player receives a unique Foundation character with special powers
- **Game Clock**: Track the duration of your psychohistorical calculations
- **Two Game Modes**:
  - Local multiplayer (pass-and-play with auto-switching)
  - Single player vs intelligent AI Mentalic
- **Enhanced AI**: Strategic opponent that understands scoring zones and uses abilities
- **Foundation Theme**: Deep integration with Asimov's Second Foundation concepts

### Setup

- 33 cards (3 suits √ó 11 cards each)
- Each player receives 13 cards
- The Prime Radiant card determines the trump suit

### Suits (Aspects of Psychohistory)

- **Mental** (Blue üß†) - Psychic manipulation
- **Physical** (Gold ‚öîÔ∏è) - Economic and military forces
- **Temporal** (Red ‚è≥) - Historical momentum

### Special Card Abilities

- **1 - Whispered Redirection**: Lose trick but lead next
- **3 - Mental Static**: Exchange a card with the Prime Radiant
- **5 - Intuitive Leap**: Draw 1, then discard 1
- **7 - Conversion Point**: Worth 1 bonus point to winner
- **9 - Mentalic Resonance**: Becomes trump when alone
- **11 - Imperial Decree**: Force opponent to play 1 or highest

### Scoring (The Subtlety Paradox)

| Tricks Won | Points | Description                                                |
| ---------- | ------ | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| 0-3        | 6      | **Subtle Influence** - Perfect Second Foundation technique |
| 4          | 1      | **Detected** - Your touch was noticed                      |
| 5          | 2      | **Obvious** - Resistance builds                            |
| 6          | 3      | **Contested** - Equations in flux                          |
| 7-9        | 6      | **Calculated** - Mathematical perfection                   |
| 10-13      | 0      | **Exposed** - Manipulation revealed, calculations void     |

First to 21 points guides the Second Foundation through the crisis.

## üß† Foundation Characters

Each player is assigned a legendary Foundation character with unique abilities:

### Available Characters

- **Hari Seldon**: Cannot be targeted by Speaker's Command (11)
- **The Mule**: Gains +1 point when winning 7-9 tricks (perfect control zone)
- **Bayta Darell**: Immune to conversion - opponent gains no points from 7s in tricks you win
- **Hober Mallow**: Trade mastery provides strategic advantages
- **Magnifico Giganticus**: Deceptive abilities that confuse opponents
- **Preem Palver**: Enhanced mentalic powers
- **Wanda Seldon**: Advanced psychohistorical calculations
- **Stettin**: Political manipulation expertise

Character abilities add asymmetric strategy - each game feels unique based on the character matchup!

This game draws inspiration from Isaac Asimov's Foundation trilogy, particularly:
